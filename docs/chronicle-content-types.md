
# Chronicle Content Types
Version 1.0

Chronicle is a historical knowledge system built around interconnected records.
Each record type represents a different aspect of history.

---

# Event

Events represent specific historical occurrences.

Examples:
- Raid on Lindisfarne
- Battle of Edington
- Sack of Paris

Events power:
- timeline
- map markers
- scenario inspiration

---

# Culture

Cultures describe societies or peoples.

Examples:
- Vikings
- Anglo‑Saxons
- Franks
- Irish Kingdoms

Typical content:
- society
- warfare traditions
- architecture
- equipment

---

# Person

Important historical individuals.

Examples:
- Alfred the Great
- Harald Hardrada
- Rollo

Person entries link strongly to events and campaigns.

---

# Campaign

Campaigns represent multi‑event historical narratives.

Examples:
- The Great Heathen Army
- Viking Raids in Francia
- Norman Conquest

Campaign entries help users follow history as a story.

---

# Arms & Warfare (Technology)

Entries describing weapons, ships, tactics, and military innovations.

Examples:
- Viking Longship
- Shield Wall
- English Longbow
- Frankish Cavalry

These are extremely useful for wargamers.

---

# Place

Important historical locations.

Examples:
- York (Jorvik)
- Dublin
- Lindisfarne

Place entries may appear on the map even without a specific event.

---

# Chronicle Philosophy

Chronicle is not merely a list of events.

It is a connected historical record where:

- places
- people
- cultures
- events
- technologies

are woven together into a single narrative of the age.
